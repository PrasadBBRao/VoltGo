# 11. UI/UX Design Guidelines

## Product Vision for Experience

VoltGo should feel premium, calm, and highly trustworthy. The interface must communicate reliability and convenience while remaining simple enough for first-time users.

## Design Principles

### 1. Clarity
Users should immediately understand where they are, what they can do, and what happens next.

### 2. Confidence
Important flows such as booking and payment should feel secure and well-guided.

### 3. Minimal Friction
The interface should avoid unnecessary steps and reduce decision fatigue.

### 4. Consistency
Shared patterns such as buttons, forms, and status indicators should behave predictably.

### 5. Accessibility
The product should be usable by a broad range of users, including those relying on keyboard navigation and screen readers.

## Visual Direction

- Modern, clean interface with a focus on high contrast and readability
- Deep blue and electric green accents to reinforce the EV and technology identity
- Rounded surfaces and soft shadows for a premium feel
- Strong emphasis on action states such as available, booked, confirmed, and payment-successful

## Core UI Patterns

### Customer Experience
- Hero section with strong value proposition
- Search and booking workflow with progress indicators
- Station detail cards with transparent pricing and status
- Clean confirmation screen with QR display and summary details

### Admin Experience
- Dashboard with key metrics and operational status
- Tables for stations, chargers, bookings, and transactions
- Filtering and search controls for operational oversight
- Clear action states for edits, toggles, and approvals

## Interaction Guidelines

- Show progress in multi-step processes.
- Provide immediate feedback for loading and success states.
- Make errors actionable and easy to recover from.
- Use confirmation dialogs sparingly and only where necessary.

## Why These Guidelines Matter

A premium product experience is not just visual; it directly affects trust, conversion, and customer retention. The design language should support both usability and brand perception.
